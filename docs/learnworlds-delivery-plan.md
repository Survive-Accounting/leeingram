# LearnWorlds Delivery Plan

## Purpose

LearnWorlds is the platform used to deliver Survive Accounting course content to students.

The Teaching Asset Pipeline produces structured learning materials that are later deployed into LearnWorlds courses.

This document explains how teaching assets translate into student-facing lessons.

---

# Student Learning Environment

Students interact with Survive Accounting primarily through LearnWorlds courses.

Each course corresponds to a specific accounting class, such as:

• Financial Accounting (Intro 1)  

• Managerial Accounting (Intro 2)  

• Intermediate Accounting 1  

• Intermediate Accounting 2  

Courses are structured by **chapter**, mirroring the organization of most accounting textbooks.

---

# Lesson Structure

Each chapter in LearnWorlds contains multiple lessons.

Lessons are built from teaching assets generated by the pipeline.

A typical lesson may contain:

• problem walkthrough video  

• embedded worksheet or whiteboard  

• practice quiz  

• explanatory notes

The goal is to simulate the experience of **working through a problem with a tutor**.

---

# Teaching Asset → Lesson Mapping

Each teaching asset can produce multiple learning elements.

Examples:

Teaching Asset Components

• Problem explanation  

• Worked solution  

• Journal entries  

• Concept notes  

• Formulas  

• Exam traps

These components can power several types of lesson content.

---

# Video Content

Instructional videos are recorded using the Google Sheet whiteboards generated by the pipeline.

Videos typically include:

• problem explanation  

• step-by-step solution  

• concept clarification  

• discussion of common mistakes

Videos are uploaded to LearnWorlds and linked to the corresponding teaching asset.

---

# Practice Quizzes

Many teaching assets can also generate practice quizzes.

These quizzes allow students to test their understanding of the concept demonstrated in the lesson.

Quizzes are created within LearnWorlds.

---

# Embedded Worksheets

In some cases, Google Sheets may be embedded into LearnWorlds lessons.

These sheets allow students to:

• follow along with the solution  

• explore calculations  

• review structured explanations

However, sheets are primarily used internally for teaching and recording.

---

# Course Navigation

Courses are typically organized as follows:

Course  

→ Chapters  

→ Lessons  

→ Practice Materials

This structure mirrors the way accounting textbooks are organized.

---

# Campus-Specific Routing

Different universities may use:

• different course codes  

• different textbooks  

• different chapter orders

The platform may eventually support a **"Choose Your Campus"** experience.

Students select their university and are directed to the correct course version.

---

# Access Control

LearnWorlds manages student access to content.

Access may be granted through:

• course purchases  

• subscriptions  

• bundled programs  

• organizational licenses

Stripe handles the payment processing.

---

# Future Enhancements

Possible future improvements include:

• automated lesson creation from teaching assets  

• automated quiz generation  

• dynamic course routing by campus  

• integrated analytics for student performance

---

# Design Principles

The delivery system should prioritize:

• clear lesson structure  

• simple navigation  

• strong alignment with tutoring explanations  

• consistency across courses

The pipeline should make it easy to produce new lessons quickly.
