# VA Roles and Pipeline Handoffs

## Purpose

Survive Accounting uses a small team of virtual assistants (VAs) to operate the Teaching Asset Pipeline.

Each role is responsible for a specific stage of the pipeline. This structure allows the system to scale while maintaining clear accountability and consistent output quality.

The goal is to keep tasks **simple, repeatable, and well-defined** so work can move smoothly between roles.

---

# Core VA Roles

The pipeline currently uses three main roles:

• Content Creation VA  

• Sheet Prep VA  

• Lead VA / Content Pipeline Manager

Each role handles a different stage of the pipeline.

---

# Content Creation VA

## Primary Responsibility

Create and prepare teaching assets before they are turned into teaching whiteboards.

This role focuses on **content preparation inside the Teaching Asset Pipeline system**.

---

## Core Tasks

• Import source accounting problems  

• Generate problem variants  

• Review generated variants for correctness  

• Verify problem logic and numbers  

• Submit assets for approval  

---

## Output

A **completed teaching asset** stored in the Teaching Asset Library.

Once approved, the system generates the Google Sheet template for the next stage.

---

# Sheet Prep VA

## Primary Responsibility

Prepare teaching assets for tutoring and video recording by formatting the Google Sheet teaching template.

This role focuses on **visual layout and teaching clarity**.

---

## Core Tasks

• Copy content from the Hidden Data tab  

• Format the Master Whiteboard  

• Organize journal entries  

• Write or format worked steps  

• Structure concept notes  

• Ensure the sheet is clean and readable  

---

## Output

A **fully formatted teaching whiteboard** ready for tutoring sessions or video recording.

---

# Lead VA / Content Pipeline Manager

## Primary Responsibility

Oversee the entire teaching asset production system.

This role coordinates the work of other VAs and ensures production stays on track.

---

## Core Tasks

• Assign chapters to Content Creation VAs  

• Track pipeline progress  

• Monitor asset production speed  

• Review asset quality  

• Identify workflow improvements  

• Communicate with the founder regarding pipeline status

---

# Pipeline Handoff Structure

Each stage of the pipeline hands work to the next role.

---

## Stage 1 → Content Creation VA

Input:

• textbook problems  

• source materials

Output:

• verified teaching assets

---

## Stage 2 → Asset Approval

The founder or Lead VA verifies the asset and approves it for sheet generation.

Approval triggers Google Sheet creation.

---

## Stage 3 → Sheet Prep VA

Input:

• generated Google Sheet template  

• asset data from Hidden Data tab

Output:

• completed teaching whiteboard

---

## Stage 4 → Deployment

The founder records videos and deploys the content into LearnWorlds.

---

# Communication Tools

Team communication occurs primarily through:

• Slack  

• short async updates  

• occasional coordination meetings

The goal is to minimize meetings and maintain a **clear asynchronous workflow**.

---

# Time Tracking

VA hours are tracked using **Clockify**.

This allows the founder to monitor:

• hours worked

• production speed

• cost per teaching asset

---

# Production Targets

The long-term production goal is to create teaching assets efficiently at scale.

A target cost of approximately **$1–$2 per finalized teaching asset** is desired once the system is optimized.

This cost efficiency allows the library to grow rapidly.

---

# System Design Philosophy

VA roles should always be designed around:

• clear task boundaries  

• minimal ambiguity  

• repeatable workflows  

• strong automation support

As the system improves, some tasks may become partially automated.

---

# Future Role Expansion

Additional roles may be added later, such as:

• Multiple Content Creation VAs  

• Multiple Sheet Prep VAs  

• QA Review VA

The Lead VA will eventually manage most day-to-day pipeline operations.
