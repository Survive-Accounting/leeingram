# Survive Accounting — Project Vision

## Overview

Survive Accounting is an exam preparation platform designed to help undergraduate accounting students succeed in core accounting courses.

The platform provides structured learning materials that break down complex accounting topics into clear, teachable components.

The goal is to build a **large, high-quality library of accounting teaching assets** that can be reused across courses, campuses, and semesters.

---

# Core Courses

The system focuses on four core accounting courses that are taught at nearly every university:

1. Financial Accounting (Intro 1)

2. Managerial Accounting (Intro 2)

3. Intermediate Accounting 1

4. Intermediate Accounting 2

These courses represent the highest demand for accounting tutoring and exam preparation.

---

# Teaching Asset Strategy

The platform is built around a **Teaching Asset Library**.

A teaching asset represents a single accounting problem that includes:

• problem text  

• worked solution  

• journal entries or calculations  

• step-by-step explanation  

• concept notes  

• exam traps and common mistakes  

Each asset becomes the foundation for multiple learning tools.

Assets can power:

• tutoring sessions  

• instructional videos  

• practice quizzes  

• worksheets  

• study guides

The goal is to build **hundreds to thousands of reusable assets**.

---

# Content Production System

To scale asset production, Survive Accounting uses a structured pipeline:

1. Source problem import

2. AI-assisted variant generation

3. Teaching asset review

4. Asset approval

5. Google Sheet teaching template generation

6. Sheet prep and formatting

7. Deployment into courses

This system allows the platform to produce content **efficiently and consistently**.

Virtual assistants support multiple stages of the pipeline.

---

# Learning Experience

Students interact with Survive Accounting primarily through **LearnWorlds courses**.

Within each course, students gain access to:

• teaching videos

• practice quizzes

• guided problem walkthroughs

• exam preparation materials

The goal is to simulate the experience of **working through problems with a tutor**.

---

# Google Sheet Whiteboards

Google Sheets are used internally as **teaching whiteboards**.

They are optimized for:

• tutoring sessions

• instructional video recording

• step-by-step explanations

Each teaching asset generates a structured Google Sheet template that includes:

• problem text

• journal entries

• worked steps

• formulas

• concept notes

• exam traps

These sheets serve as the core teaching interface.

---

# Campus Expansion Strategy

Survive Accounting is designed to scale across universities.

Each campus may have slightly different:

• course codes

• textbook editions

• instructor styles

The platform will support a **"Choose Your Campus"** flow where students select their university.

The system will dynamically route students to the appropriate course materials.

---

# Marketing Strategy

Growth is driven through:

• campus-specific landing pages

• partnerships with student organizations

• Greek life partnerships

• targeted tutoring support

• word-of-mouth referrals

The platform aims to become a **trusted exam preparation resource** for accounting students.

---

# Greek Organization Licensing

A future expansion includes **Greek organization licensing**.

Fraternities and sororities may purchase bulk access for their members.

This model provides:

• predictable revenue

• strong campus distribution

• easier student adoption

A self-service licensing portal may be built to manage these partnerships.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The long-term goal is to build:

• the largest structured accounting exam prep library available

• scalable systems for producing teaching assets

• a platform that can expand into additional accounting courses

The content production pipeline is designed to support **large-scale educational content generation**.

---

# Design Philosophy

The system should prioritize:

• clarity for students

• efficiency in content production

• scalability across campuses

• maintainability of teaching assets

Technology choices should support these goals.
